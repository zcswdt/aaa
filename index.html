DRaMa: Dual-Arm Robotic Fabric Manipulation
with quasi-static and dynamic primitives for rapid garment flattening

Abstract

Service robots across industrial, medical, and domestic environments face significant challenges in flattening garments, primarily due to their nearly infinite degrees of freedom and complex dynamic properties. Traditional quasi-static methods for handling fabrics require extensive interactions and are inefficient. While high-velocity dynamic actions enable efficient unfolding, they are unable to perform fine-grained adjustments.

To address this, we propose a self-supervised learning framework combining dynamic (fling) and static (pick&place, pick&drag) manipulation primitives using a dual-arm approach. This method unfolds crumpled garments efficiently without expert demonstrations or hand-labeled data. We introduce a novel factorized reward function based on garment coverage, shape matching, and smoothness. Additionally, we employ a Spatial Action Maps network, leveraging a ViT-Transformer architecture, to accurately determine dual-arm grasping points. Our approach significantly reduces interaction steps and improves fabric coverage. We conducted simulations and real-world experiments on five types of garments. Our approach quickly achieved over 80% coverage within just two actions on long sleeves, surpassing the state- of-the-art by approximately 9%, and reached 88%-92.9% coverage across all categories within five actions. Our zero-shot sim-to-real transfer has successfully been demonstrated on UR5 robots, proving the model's effectiveness on various fabrics..

Approach Overview

(a) In a workspace equipped with a dual-arm robot and a garment in an arbitrary crumpled configuration, a camera captures a top-down RGB image. (b) We perform image rotation and scaling to create a stack of transformed observations. (c) The stack of transformed observations and their positions are encoded. (d) Then, we predict the corresponding batch of value maps. The outputs of these encoders are decoded by the fling decoder, Pick & Place (P&P) decoder, and Pick & Drag (P&D) to generate Spatial Action Maps [15] for each primitive. (e) Batches for all primitives are concatenated to identify the highest value action. (f) The action to be executed is parameterized by the pixel with the maximum value..
